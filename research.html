Research

Building on my previous experience, I’m interested in using deep neural networks to develop more human-like models of auditory attention in my future research. In everyday life—talking with others, listening to music, or navigating busy sound environments—we effortlessly focus on certain sounds while ignoring others. Yet this seemingly simple ability is actually a very complex computational problem, and we still don’t fully understand how the brain manages to do it.

01 // SELECTED PROJECTS
Effects of Musical harmony on Neural Synchrony: An EEG Study
  • Using ten music tracks with almost same tempo (2Hz), and modulate into three superposition version (better and worse version) by Audacity, collecting subjects tapping synchrony to the beats and EEG data, then using Temporal Response Function to test the music-brain synchrony.
EEG Signatures of Perceptual Curiosity: Interactions between Information Presentation and Sound Ratio
  • Funded by Chongqing Municipal Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program, this study aims to use EEG technology, this study employed a keypress–sound paradigm to investigate the differences and neural mechanisms of perceptual curiosity under information × valence conditions.
